Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs) - Washington Accord

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs) are the most important aspect of your engineering education, aligned with the Washington Accord standards. These are high-level competencies you'll achieve upon graduation. PLOs are attained through Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs), where each CLO maps to a specific PLO. We measure your progress using Bloom's Taxonomy (K1-K6), Knowledge Profiles (K1-K8), Problem Attributes (P1-P7), and Activity Attributes (A1-A5) to ensure comprehensive skill development throughout your 4-year journey.

Bloom's Taxonomy (K1-K6)

  • K1: Remember - Recall facts and basic concepts
  • K2: Understand - Explain ideas and concepts
  • K3: Apply - Use information in new situations
  • K4: Analyze - Draw connections among ideas
  • K5: Evaluate - Justify a stand or decision
  • K6: Create - Produce new or original work

Knowledge Profiles (K1-K8)

  • K1: Natural Sciences (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)
  • K2: Math/Stats (Analytical tools)
  • K3: Engineering Fundamentals
  • K4: Specialist Knowledge
  • K5: Design Support
  • K6: Engineering Practice
  • K7: Society & Ethics
  • K8: Research Literature

Problem Attributes (P1-P7)

  • P1: In-Depth Knowledge Required
  • P2: Wide-Ranging/Conflicting Requirements
  • P3: No Obvious Solution
  • P4: Infrequently Encountered
  • P5: Outside Standards/Codes
  • P6: Diverse Stakeholder Needs
  • P7: Many Interdependent Parts

Activity Attributes (A1-A5)

  • A1: Diverse Resource Use
  • A2: Resolve Significant Conflicts
  • A3: Creative Use of Principles
  • A4: Significant Consequences
  • A5: Beyond Previous Experience
